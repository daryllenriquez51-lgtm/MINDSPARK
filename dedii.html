WHAT IS ADHD?

(Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder)

is a neurodevelopmental condition marked by excessive and widespread symptoms of inattention, hyperactivity, impulsivity, and emotional dysregulation. These symptoms can significantly impair functioning across various settings and are not suitable for the individual's developmental stage.

Approximately 8% of children and adolescents worldwide are estimated to have ADHD.

DYSLEXIA

DEFINITION

WHAT IS DYSLEXIA?

(Dyslexia)

Dyslexia is a learning disability that disrupts how your brain processes written language. People with dyslexia have trouble with reading and related skills. It’s very treatable, and it doesn’t have to stand in the way of success.

Despite these difficulties, many individuals with dyslexia possess strengths in areas such as creativity, problem-solving, and thinking outside the box.

DYSCALCULIA

DEFINITION

WHAT IS DYSCALCULIA?

(Dyscalculia)

is a specific learning disorder that affects the ability to understand numbers and perform basic mathematical operations. Individuals with dyscalculia often struggle with tasks like recognizing patterns, counting, or memorizing math facts.

Approximately 5-7% of people are affected by Dyscalculia, making it one of the most common learning disorders.

DYSGRAPHIA

DEFINITION

WHAT IS DYSGRAPHIA?

(Dysgraphia)

is a learning disorder that affects writing abilities, including handwriting, spelling, and organizing ideas on paper. Individuals with dysgraphia may struggle to produce legible and coherent written work despite having normal intelligence.

It is estimated that 5-20% of the population has some form of dysgraphia.
